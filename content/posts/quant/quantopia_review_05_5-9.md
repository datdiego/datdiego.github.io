## [Artificial Intelligence in Finance](https://www.turing.ac.uk/sites/default/files/2019-04/artificial_intelligence_in_finance_-_turing_report_0.pdf)

This is a report from the Turing Institute on how that review literature on how artificial intelligence, machine learning and deep learning applied in the financial service industry.

### First section

In the first section of this report, is a historical review of how AI evolved and was slowly applied to finance. With the most intersting applications in historical order are:
- (1957-1976) A series of papers detailing 'symbol processing hypothesis' by Newell[[1]](https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/1455567.1455605)[[2]](https://iiif.library.cmu.edu/file/Simon_box00064_fld04907_bdl0001_doc0001/Simon_box00064_fld04907_bdl0001_doc0001.pdf)[[3]](https://iiif.library.cmu.edu/file/Simon_box00066_fld05083_bdl0001_doc0001/Simon_box00066_fld05083_bdl0001_doc0001.pdf) from the RAND corporation. They try and simulate short-term memory and decision flow processes imitating human thought.

- (1990) The development of "expert systems" which is a technique that solves problems within a specific context. The Personal Financial PLanning System (PFPS) was used by Chase Lincoln First Bank and Arthur Little Inc. to perform series of financial operations and strategies, such as debt and investment planning.

- (1993) FinCEN Artificial Intelligence Systems (FAIS) was released to predict and asses money laundering, indetifying $1 billion worth of incidents over the next two years.

- (2010-2013) A series of incidents with unverified software and glitches caused billions in loses.
